\subsection{Factoring Trinomials with Leading Coefficient $\neq$ 1}

\vspace{.15in}

There are three main methods for factoring trinomials of the form $ax^2 + bx + c$ where $a \neq 1$. They are ranked below from easiest to least easy.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Slide \& Divide
\item Grouping
\item Trial \& Error
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{.15in}

My personal recommendation is the slide \& divide method. Solving these problems by grouping is valid, but still requires significant guessing and checking.
\\
A handout detailing the slide and divide method is available on Canvas.
\\

\begin{example}
Factor $5x^2 - 14x + 8$.
\vspace{1.75in}
\end{example}

\begin{example}
Factor $6x^2 + 19x - 7$.
\end{example}
